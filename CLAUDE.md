## Design Context

### Users
Impact360 is used by ministry leaders, coaches, directors, city leaders, and platform admins inside LINC and future affiliate organizations. Many users have mixed or low technical fluency and may rely on phones instead of laptops, so the interface should minimize steps, keep actions obvious, and use generous touch targets. Their core job is to submit impact data, review venture health, coordinate coaching, and communicate ministry outcomes with as little friction as possible.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel calm, confident, and mission-driven. Its voice should be warm and encouraging without becoming sentimental, and professional without feeling corporate or cold. The brand direction is modern SaaS rather than overtly church-styled, with a Linear-like sense of clarity and restraint adapted to a ministry context.

### Aesthetic Direction
Default to a light-first neutral interface with strong readability, muted stone-based surfaces, and selective use of warm amber plus supporting teal and violet accents for meaning and hierarchy. Typography should continue using a practical sans serif for UI and a restrained serif for moments of emphasis. Avoid anything that feels overly decorative, overly playful, or visually “church bulletin.”

### Design Principles
1. Make the primary flow effortless for venture leaders, especially monthly impact submission.
2. Use progressive disclosure so leadership users gain power without making simpler roles feel crowded.
3. Prefer calm clarity over visual novelty: strong hierarchy, obvious actions, restrained color, and minimal chrome.
4. Design mobile-first with large touch targets and straightforward interactions for varied technical literacy.
5. Meet WCAG AA accessibility expectations with readable contrast, clear states, and inclusive defaults.
